To get started, go to the 'Records' screen and choose from the buttons below to record an observation or start a census (koala survey). An observation is a one-off recording of a koala sighting at a particular location. A census is part of a systematic survey that is generally conducted at a defined survey site. The census method recorded by this app is the Spot Assessment Technique (SAT) (Phillips and Callaghan, 2011). ‘Trees searched’ are always part of the SAT census and record the individual tree species, at each site, that have been searched for koalas and their scats.

Once you have recorded your data, a list of the records will be displayed here. You can also switch to see the records displayed on a map view using the tab buttons above. Click on a record in the list to continue editing that record.

Uploading Records

When one or more records have been completed, you can upload them to the server by clicking the upload button. Completed records (shown in green) are those where all required fields are entered and within the allowable constraints. You can still upload completed records even if some are incomplete (shown in blue). Incomplete records will not be uploaded.

Tree surveys

When working on a census, you can create one or more tree sightings. Select the Tree surveys list tab at the top of the page and use the button below to create a new tree sighting.

Create a tree survey

GPS accuracy

The accuracy of the GPS in your device will improve over time after it has been started as it picks up signals from more satellites. You may wish to update the location information in your observation or census periodically if the current accuracy is insufficient. This can be done by pressing the "Use Current Location" button within a record form.
